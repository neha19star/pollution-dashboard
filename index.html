Noise Pollution

  • Major sources include traffic, construction, and industrial machinery.
  • Continuous exposure above 85 dB can damage hearing permanently.
  • Leads to stress, disturbed sleep, and reduced concentration levels.
  • Urban cities like Mumbai and Pune record extreme levels during festivals.
  • Government enforces strict noise regulations to reduce public disturbance.

Air Pollution

Air pollution is one of the major environmental issues affecting Maharashtra. It mainly results from vehicular emissions, industrial discharge, and burning of waste materials. This dashboard provides district-wise insights into PM2.5, NO2, and SO2 concentration levels across different regions.

  • Explore pollutant trends in different cities.
  • Understand seasonal variations in air quality.
  • Identify districts with highest and lowest AQI levels.
  • Analyze air pollution trends over time.
  • Promote awareness for clean air initiatives.

Water Pollution

  • Caused by industrial waste, sewage, and agricultural runoff.
  • Contaminates major rivers like Godavari and Krishna.
  • Leads to health issues and affects aquatic ecosystems.
  • Major urban areas face challenges in water treatment and supply.
  • Government projects focus on Clean Water Mission and pollution monitoring.

Soil Pollution

  • Soil pollution occurs when toxic chemicals or heavy metals contaminate the land.
  • Major sources include industrial waste, pesticides, fertilizers, and landfills.
  • Contaminated soil affects crops, groundwater, and overall ecosystem health.
  • In Maharashtra, urbanization and chemical industries are key contributors.
  • Proper waste segregation and organic farming can reduce soil degradation.
